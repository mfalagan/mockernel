Although Fortran *does* hold a special place in my heart, one-based indexing weirds me out. I kid, of course, but I usually do start my projects by their zeroth step. Not out of pedantry, but because it feels to me like the perfect number to give to the first (hm...) step, which usually deals with "setup & config". It will be no different with this milestone, M0, which will cover the complete set up of an environment that is fully capable of hosting the development of the project.

I, as most people, was born a Windows user, and, as most Windows users, have grown up fighting it whenever I had to get anything done. Once I discovered Docker containers, I quickly adopted them into my development stack, as a way to wiggle out of my host OS, and into a world of POSIX and rainbows. When I learned about Dev Containers, I swiftly jumped onboard. I did carry a lot of vices, though, and usually ended up spooling up a Debian container just to manually set the toolchain up. For this, my first portfolio piece, I want to take a more normative approach to the development environment.

Just to clarify, though, this is not my field. None of this is. Take whatever you see, from here on out, with a grain of salt.
